Born: 1907, Vlaardingen, Z.H., Holland.

 

career:

Came to Java in 1931 as a taxidermist of the Zoological Museum at Buitenzorg. Since 1937 he is Officer for Hunting and Nature Preservation Affairs at the Bot. Gard. Buitenzorg. In 1959 appointed Scientific Officer of the Stichting Agrarisch Onderzoek t.b.v. Ned. Nieuw Guinea (SAONNG). In charge of the investigation of the damage done by vertebrates (mainly aquatic birds) to paddy in the fields, and stationed at the Rice Plantation Koerik, W of Merauke at the mouth of the Kumbe River, in Dutch South New Guinea; from October 1962 till April 1963 stationed at the Agricultural Experimental Station at Manokwari (Vogelkop). Mid-1963 he settled at Bakkum, N.H., in the Netherlands; he occasionally visits Indonesia.

Author of mostly ornithological papers.

Author of many ornithological publications and reports with information on the vegetation.
