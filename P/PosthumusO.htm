Posthumus, Oene

 

(Source: Flora Malesiana ser. 1, 1: Cyclopaedia of collectors)

 

Born: 1898, Delfzijl, Gr., Holland. Died: c. Dec. 1945, murdered by extremists at Batavia, Java.

 

career:

Botanist, educated at Groningen University, where he took his degree on a morphological thesis, in 1924; when awarded the Buitenzorg Fund he went to the D.E.I. towards the end of 19241 and in the next year joined the Djambi Expedition for the collecting of fossil plants.2 Early in 1926 he returned to Holland, to come back in October of that year as Selectionist in the employ of the Experiment Station for the Java Sugar Industry at Pasoeroean. Since 1939 Director of the General Experiment Station for Agriculture at Buitenzorg; in 1941 also Professor at the Agricultural College, Buitenzorg.

He specialized in ferns and is the author of numerous publications; together with C.A. Backer he wrote a Fern Flora of Java (in Dutch; 1939).

Sarcanthus posthumii J.J.Sm. was named after him.

collections:

Principally ferns.4 Herb. Groningen [now in L]: 261 nos Java collection 1925; also fern dupl. The Djambi (Centr. Sumatra) collection wholly in Herb. Bog. [BO]: > 600 nos (451-1098, partly collected by Saimoendt, numbered in the same series). Collections since 1926 in private herbarium, for the greater part dupl. in Herb. Bog. [BO], Herb. Pasoer. (also some dupl. Java 1925) [PAS now in BO], Herb. Leiden [L] (365 nos, pres. 1933-35), Herb. Sing. [SING] (5 nos from Flores, pres. 1935), Herb. Geneva [G], Brisbane, etc. The total amount of his collections in Herb. Pasoer. [PAS now in BO] is c. 2000 nos, of which more than 1000 collected together with C.A. Backer (see there).

His collections from the tropics are numbered from 1 onwards; from the Lesser Sunda Islands and Celebes in 1932, the nos 3001-3504.
